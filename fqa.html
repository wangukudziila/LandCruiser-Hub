Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between LandCruiser and LandCruiser Prado?

The LandCruiser Prado is a smaller, more affordable version of the full-size LandCruiser. While both are capable off-road vehicles, the full-size LandCruiser offers more power, more interior space, and greater towing capacity. The Prado is more suited for urban driving while still maintaining strong off-road capabilities.

What is the towing capacity of a LandCruiser?

Towing capacity varies by model and year. Recent LandCruiser 300 Series models can typically tow between 3,500kg to 3,800kg when properly equipped. Always check your specific model's specifications and ensure you have the proper towing equipment installed.

How often should I service my LandCruiser?

Toyota recommends servicing your LandCruiser every 10,000 km or 6 months, whichever comes first. However, if you frequently drive in severe conditions (dusty environments, extreme temperatures, heavy towing), more frequent servicing may be required.

What fuel does a LandCruiser use?

This depends on the engine:

  • Diesel models (most common) use diesel fuel
  • V8 petrol models typically require 95 RON premium unleaded
  • Some older models may run on regular unleaded (91 RON)

Always check your owner's manual for the specific requirements of your vehicle.

Are LandCruisers good for off-roading?

Yes, LandCruisers are renowned for their off-road capabilities. They feature:

  • Advanced 4WD systems
  • High ground clearance
  • Robust suspension
  • Locking differentials (on many models)
  • Crawl Control and Multi-Terrain Select (on newer models)

This makes them one of the most capable off-road vehicles available.

What is the fuel economy of a LandCruiser?

Fuel economy varies by model and engine type:

  • Recent V8 diesel models: ~9-12L/100km (highway), ~12-15L/100km (city)
  • V6 turbo diesel (300 Series): ~8-10L/100km (highway), ~10-13L/100km (city)
  • Petrol models typically consume 20-30% more fuel

Actual consumption depends on driving conditions, load, and driving style.
